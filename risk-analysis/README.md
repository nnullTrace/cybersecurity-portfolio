# 🟡 Risk Assessment Case Studies (GRC Perspective)

## 🧠 Overview

This project presents a set of governance-focused case studies analyzing different types of risk across real-world scenarios.

The focus is on:

* Risk identification
* Governance gaps
* Business impact
* Control alignment

Rather than technical implementation, this analysis emphasizes structured decision-making and risk visibility.


## 🎯 Scope

The project includes three main risk scenarios:

### 1. External Data Exposure

* Third-party handling of employee data
* Lack of clear ownership, consent, and retention control
* Risk of privacy violations and reputational damage


### 2. Insider Risk (Proprietary Materials)

* Unauthorized access and potential misuse of internal teaching materials
* Misalignment between role-based access and actual responsibilities
* Weak monitoring and inconsistent access control


### 3. Operational Risk

* Time-based service restrictions impacting customer experience
* Controls implemented without demand analysis
* Negative impact on revenue and customer satisfaction


## 🔍 Approach

Each case follows a structured risk analysis model:

* Context
* Business objective
* Risk statement
* Key risk drivers
* Impact assessment
* Governance gaps
* Control recommendations


## ⚖️ Key Insights

* Lack of governance can create risk even without a confirmed breach
* Insider risk often comes from process gaps, not malicious intent
* Not all controls reduce risk - some introduce new business risks


## 🛠️ Frameworks & Concepts

* Impact × Likelihood risk scoring
* Policy and control alignment
* Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)
* Third-party risk management

## 🎯 Focus

* Risk visibility
* Governance and accountability
* Business-aligned security thinking

## ⚠️ Disclaimer

All cases are based on real observations and have been anonymized for professional and educational purposes.
